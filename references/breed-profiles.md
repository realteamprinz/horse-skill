# Horse Breed Profile Reference

## How Breed Data Is Used

Horse breeds have been selectively bred for centuries for specific purposes. Breed data is used to:
1. Set baseline expectations for temperament and athletic ability
2. Understand breed-typical behaviors vs individual personality
3. Flag breed-specific health concerns
4. Match expectations to discipline suitability

## Common Breed Profiles

### Thoroughbred
- Origin: England, bred for racing
- Height: 15.2-17 hands
- Temperament: Sensitive, forward, athletic, can be hot/anxious
- Best For: Racing, jumping, eventing, dressage, polo
- Energy: High — needs regular work and turnout
- Health Watch: Leg injuries (bred for speed not durability), gastric ulcers (very common), exercise-induced pulmonary hemorrhage
- Notable: Many OTTBs (off-track Thoroughbreds) available for adoption — need careful retraining

### Quarter Horse
- Origin: USA, bred for ranch work and short sprints
- Height: 14.3-16 hands
- Temperament: Calm, willing, versatile, sensible
- Best For: Western disciplines, trail, ranch work, beginners
- Energy: Moderate — steady and reliable
- Health Watch: HYPP (genetic muscle disease in some lines), navicular, PSSM
- Notable: Most popular breed in the USA, over 3 million registered

### Warmblood (Hanoverian, Dutch, Oldenburg, etc.)
- Origin: Europe, bred for sport
- Height: 16-17.2 hands
- Temperament: Varies by breeding line — can range from calm to hot
- Best For: Dressage, jumping, eventing
- Energy: Moderate to high
- Health Watch: OCD (bone lesions), kissing spine, navicular
- Notable: Breeding is performance-tested, so temperament and health records often available

### Arabian
- Origin: Middle East, one of the oldest breeds
- Height: 14.1-15.1 hands
- Temperament: Intelligent, sensitive, bonds deeply, high spirit
- Best For: Endurance, trail, show, pleasure
- Energy: High — but sustainable endurance energy, not just speed
- Health Watch: SCID (genetic immune deficiency), cerebellar abiotrophy, guttural pouch issues
- Notable: Concave "dished" face, high tail carriage, incredible stamina

### Draft Breeds (Clydesdale, Percheron, Belgian, Shire)
- Origin: Various European countries
- Height: 16-19 hands (massive)
- Temperament: Gentle, calm, patient — "gentle giants"
- Best For: Driving, light riding, therapeutic programs, farm work
- Energy: Low — cold-blooded, steady
- Health Watch: EPSM/PSSM (muscle disease), scratches (feathered legs), joint issues from weight
- Notable: Easy keepers — watch weight, don't overfeed

### Pony Breeds (Welsh, Shetland, Connemara, Haflinger)
- Height: Under 14.2 hands
- Temperament: Smart (often too smart), hardy, can be stubborn/opinionated
- Best For: Children, driving, small adult riders, companions
- Energy: Varies — some are firecrackers, some are dead-broke
- Health Watch: Laminitis (EXTREMELY prone — restrict grass), Cushing's, obesity
- Notable: Easy keepers — many ponies need a grazing muzzle, do NOT overfeed

### Gaited Breeds (Tennessee Walking Horse, Icelandic, Paso Fino, Missouri Fox Trotter)
- Height: Varies by breed
- Temperament: Generally calm, people-oriented
- Best For: Trail riding, pleasure, riders with back issues (smooth gaits)
- Energy: Moderate
- Health Watch: Breed-specific — Icelandics can't be re-imported once they leave Iceland (disease protocols)
- Notable: Natural or trained gaits beyond walk/trot/canter — extremely comfortable to ride
